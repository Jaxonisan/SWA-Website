Regarding Anxiety

According to the CDC, 10.3% of students of ages 12 - 17 experience extreme anxiety. The National Institutes of Health say that almost 1 in 3 teens from ages 13 - 17 will experience an anxiety disorder at least once in that age range. At the SWA, we notice that most of the children going to our meetings are in that age range. That is why one of our goals is to help and support those with anxiety, and hopefully get to a point where our clients are no longer going through anxiety and can begin to better live their lives to the fullest. The Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration encourages teenagers to seek help from a trusted adult, counselor, or mental health support group when you are suffering from anxiety. We try to act as the support group for you, but it may still be smart to talk to your guidance counselor from time to time. All advice given from the other mental health websites is good, but here is what the SWA has to say: When dealing with anxiety, try to take a break. Go outside for 5-10 minutes and think about the good things in your life. If you can't go outside, take a break from all work and screens for a few minutes, and maybe read a good book. Than go back to the hard parts in life with a clear mind. It will be easier to do things if your mind is cleared. We understand that current issues can be the most anxiety inducing of all, so if you need any help, you can always talk about it with us, your guidance counselor, or a counselor outside of school.

Goals

The SWA has goals, but we understand that most of you do too. That is why we encourage everybody to work hard at your goals, as it is much more satisfying to accomplish a goal than to give up on it. Additionally, the SWA wants to help you reach your goals, and we will try to do whatever you need us to do to make your goals easier to accomplish. However, everybody should take goals seriously. Some people have real, serious goals and nobody should make fun of other people's goals or have any goals that aren't serious and take our time away from goals that might be more serious. We ask that you take your and other people's goals as seriously as you can.

Final Notice

Be safe, especially with COVID-19 and variants of COVID-19. We can make 2022 better than 2021 and 2020, but only if we all do our part!
